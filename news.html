Foie Gras hosts fundraiser to support inner city schools.

The restaurant kicks of the summer with a fundraiser for the San Diego Unified School District to support the arts/music program in inner city schools. The dinner included fine cuisine including: rosemary potatoes, lamb chops, and parmesan asparagus. Proceeds from the event will be used to buy instruments, art canvases, and gallons of paint. Students and their families were also invited to perform the national anthem in front of their first live audience.

In addition, there was an art show that took place after the dinner, where guests could purchase the student's art as well as request commissions from the students. The school district was able to raise over $4,000 with the event, and the schools are planning on creating a few new art classes for the high schools, as well as the elementary schools. With the help of the event, the school district will be able to buy the art supplies for the students to use in class.


Homeless Banquet in Downtown

The staff got together in order to provide the homeless people in Downtown a meal fit for kings and queens. After the meal, the guests attended a seminar to learn about career opportunities in their restaurant as well as get a hands-on lesson with a professional chef.

Guests who were interested in any of the positions, were welcomed back to the restaurant for a tour around the kitchen as well as the dining room area. Attendees learned how to cook simple dishes using only a few ingredients as well as learned how to setup for a dinner rush quickly. The restaurant also rented out a nearby home for the week so that attendees could take a hot shower, get some rest, and read up on some of the interview books that were left for them in their rooms. The restaurant is hopeful that the guests will be able to get back on their feet and back into the workforce soon.
